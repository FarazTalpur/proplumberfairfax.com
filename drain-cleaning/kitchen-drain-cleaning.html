Frequently Asked Questions

Common causes include food particles, grease buildup, and soap scum, which can accumulate over time and block the drain.

To prevent clogs, avoid pouring grease down the drain, use a drain strainer to catch food particles, and regularly flush the drain with hot water and baking soda.

Start by using a plunger or a plumberâ€™s snake. If that doesn't work, a mixture of baking soda and vinegar can help dissolve minor clogs. For persistent issues, consider using a commercial drain cleaner or consulting a professional plumber.

Chemical drain cleaners can be effective but may damage your pipes over time if used excessively. They should be used as a last resort and with caution, ideally following the manufacturer's instructions.

A garbage disposal can help reduce clogs by grinding up food waste, but it should not be considered a foolproof solution. Avoid putting hard, fibrous, or starchy items down the disposal, and always run it with plenty of water.
