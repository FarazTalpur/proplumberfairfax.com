Water pressure problems can disrupt daily life and comfort in Fairfax, VA. From sudden drops in pressure to unexpected surges, these issues often arise due to:

Frequently Asked Questions

Sudden low water pressure can be caused by various issues including blocked pipes, partially closed valves, or problems with the municipal water supply. It's best to check for any visible leaks and ensure that all valves are fully open.

To increase water pressure, start by ensuring that the main water valve is completely open. If the issue persists, consider installing a water pressure booster, cleaning clogged pipes, or contacting a professional plumber for a thorough inspection.

Different water pressure in various parts of the house is often due to clogged pipes or fixtures, or differences in pipe diameter. It's important to check for blockages in the affected areas and ensure consistent pipe sizes throughout your plumbing system.

If your water pressure regulator is faulty, you might notice fluctuating water pressure. It's essential to replace or repair the regulator promptly to avoid damage to your plumbing system. Contact a qualified plumber to assess and address the issue.

Weather changes can indirectly affect water pressure, particularly in areas with large temperature fluctuations. Pipes can expand or contract, leading to leaks or blockages that affect pressure. Insulate pipes where possible, and regularly check for any signs of wear or damage.
