Backflow occurs when unwanted water flows in the reverse direction, potentially contaminating the potable water supply. This issue is particularly concerning in Fairfax due to its mixed-use infrastructure and seasonal weather changes that can increase wear on plumbing systems.

Common Causes of Backflow:

Frequently Asked Questions

A backflow preventer is a device installed in a plumbing system to keep water from flowing backward into the potable water supply. It is crucial because it protects drinking water from contamination due to backflow, which can occur due to changes in pressure within the system.

Backflow preventers should be tested annually. Regular testing ensures they are functioning properly and effectively protecting the potable water supply from contamination.

Common signs include water leaking from the backflow preventer, strange or foul odors in the water, lower water pressure than usual, and unusual noises coming from the plumbing system.

It is generally not recommended to perform your own backflow preventer repairs unless you are a certified professional. Improper handling can lead to further issues and contamination of the water supply.

If you suspect a backflow problem, contact a licensed plumber or a certified backflow prevention technician immediately to assess and address the issue to prevent potential contamination of your water supply.
