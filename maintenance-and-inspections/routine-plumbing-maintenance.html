Frequently Asked Questions

It is recommended to schedule routine plumbing maintenance once a year to ensure your plumbing system remains in good working condition and to prevent potential issues.

Common signs include slow drains, inconsistent water pressure, unusual noises from pipes, persistent odors, and water leaks or damp spots.

Basic tasks such as cleaning drains, checking for leaks, and ensuring faucet aerators are clear can be done yourself. However, more complex tasks should be handled by a professional plumber.

To prevent pipes from freezing, insulate exposed pipes, keep the thermostat consistent, open cabinet doors to warm plumbing under sinks, and let faucets drip slightly during extreme cold.

If you discover a leak, turn off the water supply to the affected area to minimize water damage, and contact a professional plumber to inspect and repair the issue promptly.
