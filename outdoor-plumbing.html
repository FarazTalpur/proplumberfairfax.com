Frequently Asked Questions

To prevent outdoor pipes from freezing, insulate them with foam pipe covers, ensure outdoor faucets are drained and turned off for winter, and consider using heat tape for added protection during extremely cold weather.

Unclog an outdoor drain by manually removing debris near the surface, using a plunger, flushing with a garden hose, or applying a drain snake if the clog is deeper. Ensure to wear gloves and consider flushing out the drain with a vinegar and baking soda mixture for added cleaning.

Check for leaks by inspecting visible pipes and hoses for signs of damage or moisture, monitoring water pressure, and reviewing your water bill for unexplained increases. Dye tablets can also help detect toilet leaks when placed in the tank.

For a leaking outdoor faucet, start by tightening the packing nut with a wrench. If the leak persists, turn off the water supply, and replace the washer or the entire faucet assembly if necessary. Apply plumber's tape on the threads to ensure a good seal.

Outdoor plumbing should be inspected and serviced at least once a year, ideally before the winter season. Regular checks can help identify and address leaks, clogs, or other issues that could become problematic during colder months.
