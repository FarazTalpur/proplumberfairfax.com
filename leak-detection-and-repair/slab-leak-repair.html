A slab leak occurs when pipes beneath the concrete foundation of your home develop cracks or holes. These leaks can lead to significant problems, like structural damage, mold growth, and increased water bills.

Warning Signs of Slab Leaks

  • Damp or warm spots on the floor
  • Sound of running water when all faucets are turned off
  • Mold or mildew under carpets

Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a slab leak include the sound of running water when no taps are open, warm spots on the floor, cracks in walls or flooring, high water bills, and mold or mildew under carpets.

Slab leaks can be caused by pipe corrosion, poor construction, high water pressure, soil shifting, and abrasion of pipes due to water flow or contact with the concrete foundation.

Slab leaks are typically detected using specialized equipment such as electronic listening devices, thermal imaging cameras, and pressure testing to pinpoint the exact location of the leak.

Common methods for repairing a slab leak include pipe re-routing, epoxy pipe lining, or direct access repair, which involves breaking through the slab to fix the leak directly.

The cost of repairing a slab leak can vary widely depending on the complexity of the leak, the method used for repair, and regional pricing, but it typically ranges from $500 to $4000.
