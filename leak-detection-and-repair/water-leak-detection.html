Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a water leak include unusually high water bills, damp or moldy spots on walls or ceilings, the sound of running water when appliances are off, and low water pressure.

To detect hidden water leaks, you can monitor your water meter for any unexpected changes, use leak detection devices, or look for visual signs like warped flooring or discolored walls.

Various tools are available, such as electronic leak detectors, infrared cameras, moisture meters, and smart water leak sensors that alert you of leaks via smartphone apps.

Fixing water leaks promptly can prevent structural damage, reduce water bills, avoid mold growth, and conserve water, ultimately safeguarding your property and health.

Homeowners insurance typically covers water damage that is sudden and accidental, such as a burst pipe, but usually does not cover damage from gradual leaks due to wear and tear.
